## Project Overview

The **500kVA, 33/0.400kV Distribution Substation Project** was executed for the **Federal University, Gusau (FUGUS)** to support the growing power demand of academic buildings, laboratories, and administrative offices within the university campus.

The project was aimed at providing a **dedicated, reliable, and safe power supply**, reducing frequent outages, improving voltage stability, and enhancing the overall learning and research environment.

Musnaj Electrical Concept Ltd delivered the project on a **turnkey basis**, encompassing engineering design, civil works, electrical installations, testing, and commissioning.

---

## Scope of Work

The project included complete civil and electrical works required for a standard institutional distribution substation.

::tabs
  :::div{icon="i-lucide-list-checks" label="Project Components"}
  - Design and construction of a **500kVA, 33/0.400kV distribution substation**  
  - Installation of **500kVA power transformer**  
  - Construction of **reinforced concrete transformer plinth**  
  - Installation of **33kV incoming line structures and protection equipment**  
  - Installation of **LV switchgear panels and distribution boards**  
  - Installation of **earthing and lightning protection systems**  
  - Laying, termination, and jointing of **33kV and LV armored cables**  
  - **Testing, commissioning, and documentation**  
  - Final handover to **Federal University Gusau management**
  :::

  :::div{icon="i-lucide-hard-hat" label="Safety and Compliance"}
  - Compliance with **NEMSA**, **COREN**, and distribution utility standards  
  - Works executed by **certified engineers and experienced technicians**  
  - Strict application of **PPE, grounding, and lockout–tagout procedures**  
  - Joint inspections with **client and utility representatives**
  :::

  :::div{icon="i-lucide-clock" label="Timeline and Delivery"}
  - Project Duration: **9 Weeks**  
  - Start Date: **September 2024**  
  - Completion Date: **November 2024**  
  - Status: ✅ Completed and Energized  
  :::
::

## Project Impact

The project significantly improved **power reliability and operational efficiency** across the university campus.

**Key Outcomes:**
- Stable power supply to academic, laboratory, and administrative facilities  
- Reduced outages and improved voltage regulation  
- Enhanced safety and protection of sensitive equipment  
- Improved learning, research, and administrative productivity  
- Reduced dependence on backup generators  

---

## Materials and Equipment Used

- 500kVA, 33/0.400kV power transformer  
- 33kV isolators, drop-out fuses, and lightning arrestors  
- LV switchgear panels and distribution boards  
- 33kV and LV armored power cables  
- Earthing rods, copper conductors, and earth enhancement compounds  
- Reinforced concrete transformer plinth  
- Electrical testing and commissioning equipment
